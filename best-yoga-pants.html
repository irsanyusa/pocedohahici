What to look for in yoga pants

Shopping for yoga pants is all about determining your needs, preferences, and what works for you. Physical therapist and yogi Dr. Samantha Harte provides this guide to begin the process.

  • Try a few stores/retailers: Whether through recommendations or past experiences with your favorite brand, choose two or three stores with an excellent reputation and prepare to spend time in each. 
  • Test out a few fits: Try on a bunch of them—high waisted, mid-rise, low-rise, shorts, three-quarter, or full-length. 
  • Remember the purpose: Dr. Harte says to go off of what feels the best and not what looks the best, as that will inspire you to wear them again and again. Remember the types of movements you’ll be doing in the yoga pants—and test them out. These might include squats, lunges, and so on. 
  • Phone a friend: Or try them on in front of someone. Dr. Harte says a friend, partner, or even a yoga instructor can help you focus on what’s most important by asking questions, like “How do you feel in those pants?”, “Will you feel comfortable going to your favorite yoga class without worrying if they’ll fall?” and so on. 

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

What is the best yoga pant length?

Since you want to avoid a long tail on your yoga pants that could cause you to slip or fall out of a pose, picking the right length is crucial. According to Dr. Harte, the best way to figure out your yoga pant length is first to decide if you want a flared or tapered bottom. 

“If the pants are flared, it’s best to have them no lower than a sole height, so they don’t interfere with your practice and cause an accidental fall,” she explains. “If they are tapered, though, the options are vast.” 

Ultimately, she adds that the best length comes down to whatever makes you feel the most comfortable and supported so you can get out of your head and into your body. 

What is the difference between yoga pants and leggings?

Yoga pants are designed specifically for the practice of yoga, which is highly versatile and dynamic, Dr. Harte says. “Yoga challenges the body in multiple planes of motion, stretches and strengthens the connective tissue, and often induces a serious sweat,” she explains.

Leggings, on the other hand, are often form-fitting pants worn out in the world, whether in the workplace or at your favorite brunch spot. “They don’t necessarily handle large ranges of motion or heavy amounts of sweat,” she said. “Because of that, they could end up irritating the skin or ripping if worn in a yoga class. If you’re investing in a yoga practice, it is best to invest in comfortable and supportive attire that matches the demands of the task.”

How do you pick the right size yoga pants?

To determine the best size yoga pant, Dr. Harte says to start by asking around and reading reviews. “Get as much information as possible from people who have taken a diverse range of yoga classes without constantly pulling the waistline up or returning them because they were see-through or made of cheap material,” she says. “If you’re on the bold side, get in a dressing room and try out some dynamic poses with the pants on and see how you feel."
